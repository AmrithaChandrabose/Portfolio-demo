PV SINDHU

Pusarla Venkata Sindhu (born 5 July 1995) is an Indian badminton player. Considered as one of India's most successful athletes, Sindhu has won medals at tournaments such as the Olympic Games, the World Championships, and on the World Tour. She is the first and only Indian to become the badminton world champion and only the second individual from India to win two consecutive medals at the Olympic Games. She rose to a career-high world ranking of No. 2 in April 2017.Sindhu broke into the top 20 of the BWF World Rankings in September 2012, at the age of 17. She has won a total of five medals at the BWF World Championships and is only the second woman after China's Zhang Ning ever to win five or more singles medals in the competition. She represented India at the 2016 Rio Olympics, where she became the first Indian badminton player to reach the Olympic final. She won the silver medal after losing out to Spain's Carolina Marín. She made her second consecutive Olympic appearance at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics and won a bronze medal, becoming the first-ever Indian woman to win two Olympic medals.

Awards


Arjuna Award (2013)

PV Sindhu was conferred with the prestigious Arjuna Award in 2013 by the Government of India, recognizing her exceptional performance as a young badminton player, including her bronze medal at the 2013 World Championships. The award, featuring a cash prize of ₹5 lakh, was presented to her by the Sports Minister

Padma Shri (2015)

PV Sindhu was awarded the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian award, in 2015 by President Pranab Mukherjee. The star badminton player was recognized for her outstanding performance in sports, shortly after her successes in 2014. She later received the Padma Bhushan, the third-highest award, in 2020.

Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna (2016)

Indian badminton star P.V. Sindhu was conferred with the [Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award] (then named Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna) in 2016 following her historic silver medal at the Rio Olympics. It is India's highest sporting honor, recognizing her outstanding performance.

Padma Bhushan (2020)

Two-time Olympic medalist and badminton champion P.V. Sindhu was conferred the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award, in 2020 (presented in 2021) for her distinguished service in sports. A premier Indian athlete, she previously received the Padma Shri in 2015 and the Khel Ratna in 2016.

Achievements


  • Olympic Games: Silver (2016 Rio), Bronze (2020 Tokyo).
  • BWF World Championships: Gold (2019), Silver (2017, 2018), Bronze (2013, 2014).
  • Commonwealth Games: Gold (2022, Birmingham), Silver (2018 Gold Coast), Bronze (2014 Glasgow).
  • Asian Games: Silver (2018 Jakarta), Bronze (2014 Incheon).
  • Top Ranking: Reached a career-high world ranking of No. 2 in April 2017
